Education Structure

Curriculum Overview

Our comprehensive program is structured in four progressive levels

Program Curriculum Structure

Level Description: Introduces basic practical skills for simple tasks under supervision. Focuses on safety, basic tools, and workplace communication.

Typical Items:
  • Basic practical skills in a single task area
  • Introduction to workplace safety
  • Use of simple tools and equipment
  • Ability to perform supervised tasks
  • Basic communication and numeracy for workplace use

Level Description: Equips learners to perform routine tasks independently with standard tools. Emphasizes quality standards and basic technical understanding.

Core Subjects:
  • Perform tasks independently with routine problem-solving
  • Use of standard tools, machines, and procedures
  • Application of quality standards in work
  • Ability to interpret simple technical drawings
  • More advanced occupational safety practices

Level Description: Develops skilled workers capable of handling complex tasks and troubleshooting. Prepares learners to operate specialized equipment and small teams.

Clinical Rotations:
  • Perform complex technical tasks independently
  • Operate specialized machinery and instruments
  • Troubleshooting and maintenance skills
  • Application of industry standards
  • Coordination of small teams at work sites
  • Ability to follow detailed technical manuals/drawings

Level Description: Trains technicians to supervise operations and manage resources. Focuses on advanced problem-solving, workplace compliance, and team guidance.

Advanced Training:
  • Plan, supervise, and monitor technical work
  • Advanced troubleshooting and system-level understanding
  • Manage resources and workplace operations
  • Interpret detailed technical designs
  • Ensure compliance with occupational standards
  • Conduct workplace training for junior workers
